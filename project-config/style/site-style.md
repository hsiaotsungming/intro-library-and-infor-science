# Site Style

## Audience

- First-year university students.
- General adult learners who need a clear introduction to information organization.

## Tone

- Professional.
- Concise.
- Readable.
- Suitable for learning, review, and later lookup.

## Visual Direction

- Quiet and structured.
- More like an electronic textbook than a promotional website.
- Prioritize reading flow, navigation, and conceptual clarity.
- Avoid excessive decoration.
- Keep typography consistent across similar elements.
- Design for both classroom projection and mobile browsing.

## Content Presentation

- Use clear headings.
- Keep paragraphs focused.
- Support abstract ideas with diagrams, examples, tables, or images when useful.
- Prefer consistent terminology across units.
- Ensure text size and layout remain readable on desktop, projected screens, and phones.
- For images with sources, center the figcaption and use two lines: first a short image description, then `圖片來源：` plus the source and preserved link.

## Text Generation Rules

- Do not use corner quotation marks in generated site text.
- Do not add bold emphasis in generated prose.
